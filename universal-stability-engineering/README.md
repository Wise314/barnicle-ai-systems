## The Three Pillars

### üîß Pillar 1: Inverse Design

**Design systems guaranteed to be stable before construction.**

Traditional engineering designs systems, then tests them. If they fail, redesign. Expensive. Slow. Risky.

Inverse Design flips this: calculate the constraints a system must meet to guarantee stability, then design to those constraints. If the constraints are met, stability is **mathematically guaranteed**.

**Validation:** 11 systems tested. 100% accuracy. Systems meeting constraints remained stable. Systems violating constraints failed.

---

### üéõÔ∏è Pillar 2: Closed-Loop Control

**Maintain stability through continuous monitoring and graduated intervention.**

Not a single prediction‚Äîa continuous control loop. Monitor stability in real-time. Trigger interventions at hierarchical severity levels. Maintain stability through feedback control.

**Validation:**
- 10 bearing systems monitored through complete lifecycles
- **73-90% advance warning** before failure (average 86.1%)
- 100% detection rate
- Sufficient time to schedule maintenance, order parts, prevent unplanned downtime

**What 86% advance warning means:** If a bearing has 100 days of remaining life, you get warned at day 14‚Äîleaving 86 days to act.
